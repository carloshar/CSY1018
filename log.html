Web Development

This post was written by Carl.H

My experience of learning web development was very eye-opening, I knew the basics of HTML and CSS and programming wasn't too difficult the issue I found was the amount of creativity you needed to design these pages and work the code around these designs. Essentially finding a balance between simple programming and complex design but its hard to find the best of both worlds. I feel this first term has taught me to take web development much more seriously as its going to more difficult than I prepared myself for and gave me a great appreciation for the people who design the web every day.

This term was very basic programming and design but in the end that is what websites want to be, a simple and easy to use website that is quick to load for a mobile device. So the website has to be small on the programming side as you want it to load quickly but still have a great impact on the style making it appealing for users and easy to use. We also had a quick lesson where we researched some jobs to do with web development and it showed me being able to program isn't what employers are looking for they want a team player someone who encourages other to work and works hard themselves
